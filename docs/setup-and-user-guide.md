# TrackBit School — Setup & User Guide

*For directors, principals, and school administrators. No technical knowledge needed.*

TrackBit answers one question every day: **"Is my school actually teaching well, right now?"**
It tracks your academic plan against what actually happens in classrooms, shows you which
students are slipping early enough to act, and handles your staff tasks and fee collection —
while asking each teacher for **less than a minute a day**.

This guide has three parts: **A — one-time setup** (do these in order, about 2 hours),
**B — the daily rhythm** for each person in your school, and **C — what to do each term**.

---

## Who does what (the four roles)

| Role | Who this usually is | What they see |
|---|---|---|
| **Director** | Owner / principal | Everything — dashboard, planner, students, fees, staff |
| **Coordinator** | Academic coordinator / VP | All academics + dashboard (no fees) |
| **Teacher** | Class & subject teachers | Their classes, homework, sessions, tasks (no fees) |
| **Office** | Accountant / front office | Fees and tasks only (no academic data) |

Parents do **not** log in. They receive homework and weekly updates as messages on their phone
— nothing to install, nothing to learn.

> **A promise we keep everywhere:** student A/B/C support tiers are visible to staff only.
> They are never shown to parents or students, in any message or screen.

## Part A — One-time setup (Director, in this order)

Have ready before you sit down: your staff list, this year's holiday list, the syllabus
(chapter lists) per class, your student roster (an Excel file works), and your fee structure.

### Step 1 — Sign in

Open TrackBit in your browser and sign in with the credentials you were given. As Director
you land on the **Dashboard** — it will be mostly empty. By the end of this guide it won't be.

### Step 2 — Invite your team (sidebar → **Members**)

Add each staff member with the right role (Coordinator / Teacher / Office). Each person gets
an invite link; opening it lets them set their own password. You can also add people in bulk
and hand them their starting credentials.

*Tip: invite your coordinator first — they can share the remaining setup with you.*

### Step 3 — Define your academic structure (sidebar → **Setup**)

In order, on the Setup screen:
1. **Academic year** — e.g. 2026-27 (April–March), and your working weekdays.
2. **Terms** — e.g. Term 1 (Apr–Sep), Term 2 (Oct–Mar).
3. **Subjects** — English, Mathematics, Science, …
4. **Classes & sections** — 6-A, 6-B, 7-A …, each with its class teacher.
5. **For each class**: assign the subject teacher and the **periods per week** for each
   subject (from your existing timetable — TrackBit uses this to know how much teaching
   time each subject really has).
6. **Skill areas** — the areas your term-start diagnostic test measures (Reading, Writing,
   Speaking, Math…). Set these to match your own paper test exactly.

### Step 4 — Bring in your students (sidebar → **Students**)

- **Import** your roster Excel file — TrackBit reads it and shows you what it understood
  before anything is saved; you confirm the mapping and commit.
- Or add students one by one.
- For each student, add a **guardian with a phone number** — this is where homework
  notifications go.

### Step 5 — Build the year calendar (sidebar → **Planner**)

Add every holiday, exam block, and event you know about (Independence Day, half-yearly
exams, Dussehra break, annual day…). Watch the **effective teaching days** number update —
this is your real year, not the printed one. When you add an event mid-year later, the plan
adjusts automatically.

### Step 6 — Enter the syllabus (Planner → **Plan**)

For each class-subject, add the chapters and topics. You can paste a chapter list and
TrackBit will split it into topics for you — review and adjust.

### Step 7 — Draft and approve the plan (Planner → **Plan**)

Press **Draft** — TrackBit spreads the syllabus across the real teaching weeks (it already
knows your holidays and periods per week). Drag topics between weeks until it looks right,
then **Approve**. That locks the *baseline*. From now on TrackBit compares reality against
this plan and re-forecasts on its own — **nobody ever has to maintain the plan by hand.**

Every teacher now sees their own classes' week-by-week plan.

### Step 8 — Set up fees (sidebar → **Fees**)

1. Student categories (Day Scholar, Hosteller…) if you use them.
2. **Fee structures** per class + category: total fee and its installments.
3. **Enrol students** — from a structure, a custom schedule, or one lump amount.
   Opening dues from last year can be carried per student.

From now on the office records payments here; you see collection totals on your Dashboard.

### Step 9 — Operational boards (sidebar → **Boards**) — optional but recommended

Ready-made **Maintenance** and **Housekeeping** boards exist: a broken fan becomes a task
with a photo and an owner; cleaning checklists repeat automatically with photo proof. Use
**Home / Boards / Done** for all staff task work.

**Setup is done.** The next section is what each person does from tomorrow morning.

## Part B — The daily rhythm

### The teacher's day (under 1 minute per class — this is the whole ask)

1. **My Day** (their landing screen) shows today's classes, each with this week's planned
   topic already filled in, plus today's sessions.
2. After a class: open it, tap **Covered** (or "partially", or pick a different topic —
   two taps). Type, speak, or photograph the homework. Done — about 25 seconds.
3. Guardians of that class get the homework message automatically — no diary writing,
   no "what's the homework?" calls. *(Message delivery switches on once your school's
   WhatsApp line is connected; until then TrackBit records everything and is ready.)*
4. Next day: one number — "34 of 40 did the homework."
5. Running an after-school or remedial class? Open **Sessions**, tap attendance as students
   arrive (late arrivals get their minutes noted), tick homework done/not, take **one photo**
   of the pile. No report to write — ever. The record appears on the Director's dashboard
   by itself.

### The coordinator's day

- **My Day → Compliance**: see which classes weren't logged yesterday; a gentle word does
  the rest.
- Verify score entries after tests (Assessments).
- Review the plan when the Director adds events; keep the syllabus tidy.

### The director's ten minutes (sidebar → **Dashboard**)

- **Red / amber / green** for every class-subject: pace against the approved plan.
- **Alerts**: subjects falling behind, weak-subject warnings, missed logging — each with a
  **Create task** button so the problem lands on someone's desk with a deadline.
- Yesterday's **session records** — who attended the homework class, how late, who finished.
- The **fees card** — collection at a glance (only you and Office see fee data).
- The **Monday digest** — your week's top issues in one short summary.

### The office's day (sidebar → **Fees**)

Record payments (a receipt ledger is kept automatically), apply discounts, undo a mistaken
entry (the original is never deleted — a correction entry is written, so the audit trail is
complete), watch the defaulter list, and use the Maintenance board for repair requests.

## Part C — Each term

### Term start: the diagnostic

Conduct your own paper diagnostic test as you always have. Then in **Assessments**:
create a cycle (type: diagnostic), enter the scores per skill area (grid entry, or import),
coordinator verifies. TrackBit builds each child's **skill profile**.

### Banding and intervention (the heart of it)

On the **Bands** tab, TrackBit suggests A/B/C support tiers from the scores — teachers
confirm or override. For every C-tier child, set a goal ("move to B by term end") with a
daily checklist (hard words, writing practice, reading). Those checklist items become
**recurring tasks for the class teacher** automatically, and session attendance feeds the
same record. Remember: tiers are staff-only, always.

### During the term

Enter unit-test marks each cycle. Watch **Trends** — if a class average slips, a
weak-subject alert reaches you *before* results day, which is the whole point.

### Term end

Re-test, re-enter, and look at **band movement** — how many children moved C→B, B→A.
That number is your school's honest "are we educating?" score.

## Frequently asked

**Do teachers need training?** One demonstration is usually enough: My Day → tap Covered →
enter homework. If a teacher can use WhatsApp, they can use TrackBit.

**What if a teacher doesn't log?** The coordinator's compliance view shows it the next
morning. No confrontation needed — the gap is simply visible.

**Does this replace our ERP / attendance / report cards?** No, and deliberately so.
TrackBit runs *beside* your existing systems and does the one thing they don't: tell you
whether teaching is on plan and which children need help, early.

**Is our data separate from other schools?** Yes — every school's data is isolated at the
database level, enforced on every single request.
