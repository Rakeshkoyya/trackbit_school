01 The problem

Schools don't fail on strategy. They fail on the days.

An organisation fails when it stops seeing itself. When the day's data isn't recorded, the owner can't tell where the school is slipping — and what a school can measure, it can fix and grow. Yet in most schools the data piles up every single day: unorganised, mostly overlooked, and scattered across Excel sheets and WhatsApp that nobody can keep current.

The owner can't see the school. Which teachers are free right now, and what are they doing all day? Is the workload shared fairly across staff? Are the students actually growing? Are parents being kept informed? Most principals can't answer these from data — only from impressions, formed too late to act on.

Today's summary is written after the fact. Were all eight periods held? Is the syllabus on pace? The monthly report is assembled from memory and often shaped to look fine — so a class eight chapters behind is discovered a week before the exam, when nothing can be done.

The teacher has no minute to spare. The same attendance and homework get written into a register, a diary, and a WhatsApp group. If a system is slow or clumsy, the data is never captured cleanly — and nothing built on top of it can be trusted.

And the tools they bought died by March. Every one demanded data entry nobody had time for. The information ends up scattered across Excel and WhatsApp — none of it connected, none of it searchable, no single source of truth.

TrackBit solves all of this — teacher workload and availability, student growth, syllabus progress, fees and parent communication — by making the day's data effortless to record, and instantly visible to everyone who needs it.

What a school records, it can improve. What it never records, it cannot even see.

02 The core idea

Record each class in about five minutes.

Every problem above comes down to one thing: getting the day's data recorded cleanly, without turning it into a second job. TrackBit makes that recording fast, familiar and low-effort — so it actually gets done, every day.

In about five minutes a teacher records a class. Attendance works the way teachers already do it — the roster is on screen and each student is marked, the familiar roll-call moved from a paper register into the system. The daily plan is a single mark: which topic was taught today. Homework is added in the same flow. And exam results are captured just by photographing the marks sheet — the AI reads it and fills in the grid for you to check.

Once that is in, everything downstream is computed, not entered again: per-student growth, syllabus pace, the parent's view, the director's dashboard, and the daily report that writes itself.

03–11 The modules, in depth

Every module, in detail.

Each module below stands on its own, but every one reads from and writes to the same daily truth — so nothing is ever entered twice, and every screen agrees with every other.

03 · Academic planner & tracker

Plan the whole year's syllabus down to each period — and track it against reality.

You give TrackBit the syllabus and the school calendar; it compiles a period-by-period plan for every class-subject, aware of terms, holidays and real teaching days. Chapters are sized against the actual periods available, so an over-ambitious portion is flagged before the year starts, not after.

Then it holds the school to that plan. The approved plan is the baseline; the classroom log is the actual. A live red / amber / green forecast shows exactly which class-subjects are on pace and which are slipping — and an exam-fit check answers the question that matters: can this portion still be finished before the test?

Term-scoped chapters Real teaching-day math RAG pace forecast Exam-fit check Mid-year adoption
04 · Timetable

Map the timetable to every teacher, with automatic clash detection.

The timetable maps periods to classes and teachers, with a deterministic clash detector that refuses to double-book a teacher. Import it from a photo or a spreadsheet and the system reads it in; from that grid, each teacher's day assembles itself into the list of periods they'll actually teach.

It is effective-dated and append-only, so a mid-term change to the schedule never rewrites history — last month's records still reflect the timetable that was truly in force then.

Photo / spreadsheet import Teacher clash detection Effective-dated changes
05 · Classroom logs & attendance

Record attendance and the topic taught, for every class, every day.

A teacher opens the class, marks attendance down the roster the way they always have, picks the topic taught from the syllabus, and adds any homework — the whole class recorded in a few minutes. An optional deeper log lets the teacher note only the students who stood out.

The first attendance of the day feeds everything that needs it — the record a parent will later see, the follow-up a class teacher should make. Nothing is written into a register twice.

Roster attendance Topic from the plan Homework in the same flow Optional deep log
06 · Homework

Assign homework once — and parents see it instantly.

Homework is logged as part of closing the period — class-wide, or for specific students when the work is personal. Because it lands in the same system the parent can see, the teacher gets value the instant they record it: the parent is informed without a single extra message being written.

Replaces: the homework diary, the class WhatsApp group, and the "my child forgot to write it down" phone call.

07 · Parent portal

A parent login to see the day's lessons, homework, and how their child is doing.

Every parent gets a login to their child's actual day: the topic taught in each period, the homework set, and — when their child was absent — exactly which topics they missed, so the gap can be closed at home. It is the class record, in the open, and it turns the school's daily capture into the parent's transparency at no extra effort.

What parents see is the record of the class and their child's part in it. The private intervention tiers that staff use to target help are never shown to a parent — transparency where it builds trust, discretion where it protects the child.

Per-period topics Homework set Missed-while-absent Performance over time
08 · Exams & student growth

Enter or photograph exam results, and track each student's growth.

Enter exam results — by typing, or by dropping a photo of the marks sheet, which the system reads and pre-fills for review. Those scores flow into a growth view for every child: how much of each subject has actually been taught, the topics they missed while absent, their test trend over time, a skill profile, and the areas where they need help.

Behind the scenes, staff can group students into private A/B/C support tiers and spin up targeted interventions that become trackable tasks — the mechanism that turns "this child is slipping" into something the school actually does.

09 · The daily report

An automatic daily report of the whole school, ready every morning.

Overnight, TrackBit reads the whole day — attendance, topics covered, homework, syllabus pace, sessions and fees — and composes the report a principal would otherwise spend an hour assembling: the risks that need attention, the loose ends and ambiguities, and the wins worth celebrating. It is on the director's phone before the first bell.

Replaces: the Monday review deck, the WhatsApp round-up, and the hours spent chasing staff for numbers.

10 · Task management

A complete task system for the principal and staff — to organise work and track teacher performance.

A full task-management layer runs everything the school does beyond the classroom: maintenance, housekeeping, events, admin and academic follow-ups. The principal assigns work to a person or leaves it claimable on a board, sets recurring routines that materialise themselves, and sees who completed what and when — a clear, accountable view of how each teacher and staff member is performing.

And it ties straight back to the academics: every alert on the director's dashboard becomes a task in one tap, so a red syllabus forecast or a repeat absentee doesn't just get noticed — it gets owned.

Assign or claim Recurring routines Teacher performance view Alert → task in one tap
11 · Fees & the hostel

A fee management system that tracks every payment and never misses a due.

Fees handles the whole cycle: fee structures, instalment plans, discounts, payments and receipts. It shows you at a glance who has paid, who is due and who is overdue — so nothing slips through — and records every payment, refund and adjustment as a new entry rather than a silent edit, so the books always add up. Fees are director-only; teachers never see a rupee.

And for residential schools, the hostel runs on the same rails as the school day — evening prep, study blocks and activities, with rosters built automatically from class and boarder status — so a child's day is tracked from the first bell to lights-out.

12 The intelligence layer

More than a chatbot — an AI that builds new views and modules for you.

Most "AI in edtech" is a chat window stapled to the side of an old product. TrackBit's AI is the opposite: it reaches into the live school through the same permissions a human has, and it can compose the interface itself. This is the part of the product with no equivalent in the market — and the reason a school that adopts TrackBit keeps discovering new value long after go-live.

Two ideas make this real. First, an assistant that acts through your data, not beside it — it can read the plan, the attendance and the growth of any child, and answer or act within the exact permissions the person asking already holds. Second, a generative interface: instead of a fixed set of screens someone hard-coded, TrackBit assembles the right view — a table, a chart, a report card — from live data, on demand. Together, they turn the product from something a school configures once into something that keeps producing new answers, and new views, as the school asks for them.

The adaptive school OS

Ask it. And watch it build.

Underneath sits a registry of the school's real capabilities — every service the product exposes, described in a machine-readable way. The AI plans over those capabilities to answer a question or take an action, and then renders the answer as live interface: a table, a chart, a roster, a report card — chosen and assembled on the spot from real data it is not allowed to invent.

The interface is not a fixed set of screens. The AI materialises the right view from the data on demand — the numbers are always server-computed and never typed by the model. The system shows you what you asked to see, built in the moment.

Describe a new thing you want to track, and the system builds the module for you. That is the direction TrackBit is built to grow in.

Everywhere else in the product, the same doctrine holds: AI proposes, a human confirms, and deterministic rules decide. It drafts the year plan, splits a chapter into teachable topics, reads a timetable photo, and writes the daily report — but every output lands in a review surface a person signs off before anything is saved. The intelligence makes the work effortless without ever putting the school on autopilot.

13 How it all connects

Why schools actually keep using it.

The reason isn't a feature — it's the shape of the whole system. Value comes before data entry, and data entry is never a separate job.

Teachers give the least, and get the most. The only thing asked of a teacher is a few minutes per class — and even that pays them back immediately, because logging homework informs the parent and finishing a class clears their list. There is no data-entry role to hire, no clerk transcribing registers, no month-end scramble.

Everyone downstream reads one truth. The parent's portal, the director's dashboard, the growth tracker and the daily report are all views of the same captured day. Nothing is stored twice, so nothing can disagree. Fix a fact once and every screen updates.

It meets the school where it is. A school can adopt TrackBit mid-year, size chapters as each term begins, and run two campuses or a hostel — the system bends to that instead of demanding the year start over. Onboarding is done for the school, from the files it already has.

And it compounds. Every day of capture makes the growth history richer, the forecasts sharper, and the AI's answers more grounded. The product a school uses in year two is measurably more valuable than the one they started with — without a single upgrade to buy.

14 Trust & architecture

Built to keep your data accurate, secure and private.

Tenant isolation, enforced at the database.

Every school's data is scoped by a verified identity on every request and fenced by row-level security in the database itself — so a query can never reach across schools, even by mistake. Multi-school operation is a first-class design, not a bolt-on.

Append-only history — undo, never overwrite.

Fees, plan approvals, band changes and score verifications are recorded as new entries, not silent edits. An undo is a compensating entry. The books and the record can always be audited, and nothing quietly disappears.

Bands are private by design.

The A/B/C support tiers exist only to target help. They are staff-only and, as a hard product rule, never appear on any parent-facing surface. Discretion is enforced in the architecture, not left to policy.

AI that can't fabricate.

The assistant renders only server-computed data, and any action it proposes is a confirmable pending step, not a silent write. The model chooses how to present the truth; it is structurally prevented from inventing it.

16 Where this goes

Where the product is going.

TrackBit today already runs the daily loop of the school — plan, classroom, child, parent, money — from one source of truth. The trajectory is to make that operating system adaptive: a platform where a school describes a new thing it wants to track and the system composes it, where the AI moves from answering questions to assembling whole modules, and where every additional school and every additional day makes the intelligence sharper for everyone.

The moat is not any one feature — competitors can copy a screen. It is the combination that is hard to assemble: a capture mechanism teachers actually sustain, a single connected truth underneath, and an AI layer that can reshape the product itself. That combination is what turns software a school merely buys into the system a school runs on.

Each of those is hard to build alone; together they compound. Every school that joins and every day of capture makes the plan-vs-actual sharper, the growth history deeper and the AI more grounded — so the product a school runs in year three is meaningfully more valuable than the one it started with, and the distance from anything a competitor can assemble only widens.
